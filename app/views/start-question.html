What is the Supplier Information Service?

The Supplier Information Service was established by the Procurement Act 2024 with the aim of reducing the duplication of information submitted by suppliers when bidding for public sector work.

Contracting authorities must obtain core information about a supplier from the Supplier Information Service for any above threshold procurement. This service enables suppliers to update their core information and share it with contracting authorities by generating a code that can be shared with them, or by allowing you to download your data and send it to them manually.

When to use it

When bidding on an above-threshold procurement, you must submit your core information using the Supplier Information Service.

Updating your information

You must first register on the central platform. Once registered, you can access the Supplier Information Service. The 2024 Procurement Act describes the “mandatory core” information as the data that is required (and is relatively unchanged) in every procurement. This is equivalent to parts 1 and 2 in the current standard questionnaire (SQ).

Bidding for a procurement opportunity

Check your core data. You should ensure that it is up-to-date and correct any errors or expired information. Click ‘share’ on the home page. This will give you the option to create:

  1. A code that can be shared with eSender portals to give them access via an API to the specific dataset that you have authorised.
  2. An extract of your data. This will download a copy of the data in machine-readable (JSON) and human-readable (PDF) formats to your computer. This can be used when the contracting authority is not using an eSender for the procurement, or where they have specified a manual data upload.
  3. Both a code and an extract.
  4. Complete the declaration with your details. Once complete, your code will be displayed or your download can be accessed
  5. Share your code with the contracting authority’s eSender or upload the data file.

    6. Note -to complete your bid, you must also submit your bid-specific information by whatever method the contracting authority specifies.
